    MISSION AWARENESS COMMUNITY
    What We Do

    (UN)HEARD is a resource and awareness platform dedicated to documenting and addressing the social isolation experienced by Deaf and Hard-of-Hearing (D/HH) individuals. We compile research, amplify lived experiences, identify systemic barriers, and provide actionable solutions to foster genuine inclusion.

    Through data-driven insights and personal narratives, we create a comprehensive picture of how communication barriers, social stigma, and systemic gaps contribute to isolation—and more importantly, how we can dismantle these obstacles together.

    "Social isolation isn't just about being alone—it's about being present but unheard, surrounded but disconnected, speaking but not understood."
    Why It Matters

    466 million people worldwide live with disabling hearing loss, yet their experiences of social isolation remain largely invisible to the hearing majority. D/HH individuals face 28% greater odds of social isolation compared to their hearing peers, with 60-65% of D/HH youth reporting increased loneliness.

    This isn't just statistics—it's daily reality. It's the teenager who stops attending social events because group conversations move too fast. It's the professional passed over for promotions despite their qualifications. It's the older adult who withdraws from community life after developing hearing loss.

    Social isolation leads to increased depression, anxiety, and cognitive decline. But it doesn't have to be this way. By raising awareness, challenging misconceptions, and advocating for systemic change, we can create a world where D/HH individuals are truly heard—not just accommodated.

    Who We Serve

    D/HH INDIVIDUALS: This platform is for you—to see your experiences validated, find community resources, and access tools to combat isolation. Your voice matters here.

    HEARING ALLIES: We provide education to help you understand D/HH experiences, recognize barriers you may not see, and learn how to create more inclusive spaces in your communities and workplaces.

    EDUCATORS & PROFESSIONALS: Access research, best practices, and resources to support D/HH students, clients, and colleagues more effectively.

    POLICYMAKERS & ADVOCATES: Use our data and insights to inform accessibility policies, workplace accommodations, and systemic reforms that reduce isolation.

    Contribute Your Voice

    This project grows stronger with community input. Share your story, provide feedback, or connect with us about collaboration opportunities. Whether you're D/HH yourself, a family member, educator, or ally—your perspective helps build a more complete picture.

    Every story shared, every barrier identified, and every solution implemented brings us closer to a world where no one is (un)heard.
